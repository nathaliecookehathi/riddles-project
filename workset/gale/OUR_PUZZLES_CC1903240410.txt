The first and last words read across from left to right spell letters. G. S. S. Our Competition for August, PRIzE BOOKS FOR PRIZE LETTERS. All boys and girls write letters nowadays, so that this competition will be an easy task for all who take part in it. Perhaps the only difficulty will be to decide what to write, not because you can find little to write about, but on account of the impossibility of crowding all the things you want to say into a letter of 200 or 300 words. Write about your holidays-your favourite games-your dumb friends-your favourite books -your favourite lessons-whateyer you like best and can write best about. But, remember-the writing, spelling, and the composition will be taken fully into considera- tion-your success or your failure will not depend alone upon the contents of your letter. A prize is offered to readers under 15 years of age for the best letter containing about 200 words-not more. A prize will also be awarded to the reader over 15 years of age who sends the best letter not exceeding 300 words in length. Each letter sent in must be in every respect unaided work; have the words " For Competition" at top of page 1; the full name, age, and address, and, if the writer resides in the British Isles, must reach the Editor of Gleanings, 146, Queen Victoria Street, E.C., before or by Septem- ber 7th, 1899. Each competitor is entitled to send one letter. Note.-Two prizes are offered under the same conditions to readers residing outside the British Isles-their letters must reach the Editor'before or by October 10. Any prize may be withheld at the discretion of the Editor if competitions are comparatively few or below the average merit.
