Most boys and girls who take in the Gleanings have learned that they ought not to read " every- day books" on Sunday. We must try to keep this first day of the week a "white day," un- spotted from the world, so that even what we read shall be " on the side of the angels." Now it may not always prove easy to draw a sharp line, and to say the books on this shelf are Sunday ones, and the books on that shelf are not. Yet there is a deep, true instinct which will guide Christians-even young Christians-in this matter, if they honestly try to be guided. I want to find out which are your favourite " Sunday books," and the best wany to discover this is to take a vote. So I invite each of you who has not left school, and any other readers who are still under eighteen, to join in this competition. Write on a postcard the names of the eight Sunday books which you like, in the order in which you lilce them best, excluding the Bible, the Prayer Book, and Hymn Books. Add your name, age,
and address, and post the card to the Editor of Gleanings, 146, Queen Victoria Street, London, so that he gets it not later than June 10th. Then, in the July Gleanings, the state. of the poll will be published, and each of you will be able to see how far your own private taste corresponds with the taste of the majority. In this way, too, you may, perhaps, hear of some excellent Sunday books which you have never read. We offer a prize to every one who sends in a list which corresponds accurately, both in names and in order, with the eight books at the head of the poll.
