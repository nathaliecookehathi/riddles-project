"THE RIDDLE THAT MADE COUNT BISMARCK SICK."-It is said that the following riddle, which was set in circulation by an emissary of the Austrian Government, was the real cause of Count Bismarck's illness. The riddle is this: In Prussia proper (that is, in old Prussia, and not in any of the new pro- vinces sewed on to he kingdom by the needle-gun) there lived a blind bteggar; that blind beggar had a brother; that brother died; the man who died had no brother ; what relation, then, was the blind beggar to the man that died? This was too much for Bismarck; nor were his feelings alleviated when it was suggested that the " blind beggar" might have been the sister "of the man who died," which was, in fact, the simple truth of the matter,
