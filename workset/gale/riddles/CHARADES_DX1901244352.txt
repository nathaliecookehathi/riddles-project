CHARADES. I.
WHEN angry winds torment the swelling main, My first will be the place you wish to gain; But for myfirst you'll shed a sorrowing tear, Unless my second shall the first appear. But though my second you with joy may see, And at my first in safety hope to be, Avoid my whole, it oft has prov'd the grave Of females lovely and of seamen brave. II. What is that which, when the morning Scares away the grey-eyed night, Ever hails the cheerful dawning, As it takes its early flight ?- 'Tis my first ; my second ever You may find amidst the chace : Now, my readers, if you're clever, Find a flower full ofgrate.
