CHARADE.
I've lived in deeds of war and strife In preaching peace I've spent my live; I sometimes run, but often walk, And without tongue can swiftly talk ; The women I love, but the men I despise, I am fairly beset by the fair creatures' sighs ! My lot is a poor one, unblest and alone; Now, tell me my name, for to you I am known. J-n Q- n.
