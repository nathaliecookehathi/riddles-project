Result of July Prize Competition. The task of " Text Choosing" attracted a large number of competitors, whose ages range from 10 to 68 years! Between three and four hundred lists have been received. Only three of these came from boys. A few gave initials only, instead of names; some were over-age, or omitted to enter age, or in other ways dis- regarded the order of the rules. About twenty sent in texts for the whole of the twelve subjects (372 texts each). The word "Peace" was far more widely chosen than the other subject words-" Time," "Cloud " and "Clouds," " Sow " and "Sown," " Rain," " Sun," " Labour," "Sing," "Harvest," "Joy," " Sorrow," "Help." "Sorrow" comes last of all, with only eighteen lists. Quality is not as a rule indicated by quantity. There are signs of insufficient care and judgment in some cases, evidently due to the attempt to accomplish too much. Less work done, with more caution, and not quite so hurriedly written, would have given a more pleasing proof of ability. Nevertheless, very creditable results have been sent in by most of the competitors, and the task of awarding the prizes involved considerable diffi- culties. The twelve copies of the Bible Atlas-are awarded to the following competitors :-Margaret R. Bell (16); Alice G. Farrar (19); Ethel S. Crowther (17); Ethel M. Challenor (19); Bessie Griffiths (19); Florence Doig (22); Hetty Carter (17) ; Ruth Luxford Stewart (22); Eleanor Maria White (16); Nessie H. Bell (18); Eva Mary Taylor (20); Miriam Hill (17). A New Testament is also awarded as an extra prize to Alice G. Farrar for the special excellence of her work. The following competitors also deserve honour- able mention for their meritorious and pains- taking lists:-Lilian Hicks (17); Emily E. Westcott (16); Florrie Westcott (17); Edith Archer (17); Aminta Gomes Casseres (16); Ada Godden (17) ; Annie Hill (17). The Editor has great satisfaction in finding how popular and successful, in the best sense of the word, this competition has proved. One young servant who has won a prize writes :- " We have no concordance here, so we have to search our Bibles, and it has taken a long time, but we like doing it." Another letter says:- "You have set a delightful task. May it prove profitable to all engaged in it !" Competitors may observe some of the fruits of their work if they examine our new Penny Sheet Almanac for 1900.
