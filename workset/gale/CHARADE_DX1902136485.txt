CHARADE.
My head is large, my feet are small, My body wide, no heart at all; Some teeth are black, as.black as jet, Whilst others are white as ivory; yet What is most strange, I cannot speak, Though all to hear my notes will seek, As sweeter than most birds can sing, The emblem of each joyous thing. Now all may guess my name I ween, From Grisi fair to England's queen. M.
